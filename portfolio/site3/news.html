Wattle Flour Mill

Wattle Flour the local flour mill was established in 1912 and sourcing from local farmers, it serviced both local business and exported internationally to Bangkok Thailand, Rangoon (now Yangon, Myanmar) and Java. The original mill was sadly destroyed by a fire in 1943 halting industry until a more modern mill was erected in the same location. Read about it here inlcuding the names of those who worked there. Wattle Flour Mill.

AIF Soldier of the Bland

Find your World War I hero Our research officer Pam Butcher has completed and compiled research for a personal database of 750 AIF Soldiers who were born, lived and/or died in the Bland Shire. Contact Pam Butcher directly for a free index. Additional, detailed information & further research of an individual soldier is available for a fee. Email : butcher.pam@yahoo.com

Name Lists & Surname Research

The WDFHG undertake Name searches, based on the surnames of families who have been permanent residents in the Bland Shire and/or who have contributed to the community and its surroundings. WDFHG Member Surname Interest provides the current list of surnames of interest to our members and the associated places and dates.

The Advocate

Local residents and members of the WDFHG are helping to contribute to the transcription of local newspapers now available on the Trove website. This work is open to the public and inscription can be made directly on the Trove website.
