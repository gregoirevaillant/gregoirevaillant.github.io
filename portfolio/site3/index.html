CAN YOU HELP? Do you have an ancestor in an unmarked grave?
On one of our recent visits were were happily surprised to find a brand new headstone on a previously unmarked grave, Sarah and Arthur CARTER who died in 1900 & 1896, respectively.

With our ongoing projects connected to the Wyalong Cemetery and Tales from the Grave books, we continue to notice a number of unmarked graves in several sections of the cemetery.

The Wyalong District Family History Group Inc.(WDFHG) has been serving historians and genealogists in their quest for historical, genealogical and research information, within our local region since 1994.

Our archives include available registers, lists and resources of towns listed in the Bland Shire. All resources, events & workshops are accessible to both members & non-members.

YESTERDAY, TODAY & TOMORROW Wyalong is a town full of fascinating stories and even though it is located far from the capital cities and coast line it still manages to be an essential part of our Australian industry and history. We have included some of our Local Business stories here in Current News. You can also visit out Facebook Page with additional information about our events and projects.

“The Bland Shire, home to the Wiradjuri people, was first explored in 1817 and the first squatters settled in 1833. The area
quickly diversed after the discovery of gold in 1893, resulting in a centralised settlement.”
